## Project 1: Full Autonomous AI Outreach System

Most freelancers spend hours every week hunting for clients — manually searching Google, digging through websites, copy-pasting emails, writing the same cold pitch over and over. We built a system that does all of that automatically, every single day, without anyone touching a keyboard.

### Phase 1 — The Lead Scraper Bot

The foundation. A Telegram bot you can talk to like a person.

You send it a message: "Find me 10 marketing agencies in Austin." It understands what you mean, fires up a headless browser (a real Chrome browser running invisibly in the background), navigates to Google Maps, scrolls through results, clicks into each business profile, and extracts the name, address, website, rating, and email — all while you're watching a two-minute timer. When it's done, it replies with the full list and saves everything to an Airtable database in real time.

It also lets you search what's already been collected — "Show me all leads in London with a rating above 4" — and it queries the database and replies instantly.

That was Phase 1. Useful. But still manual. You still had to ask it to do things.

So we kept going.

### Phase 2 — The Autonomous Outreach Engine

This is where it gets serious.

Every morning at 8:00 AM UTC, without anyone pressing a button, the system wakes up on its own. It picks a city and a business category from a rotating list spanning 18 countries — USA, Canada, UK, Australia, New Zealand, Netherlands, Germany, UAE, Ireland, Singapore, and more — and starts working.

**Step 1 — Discover**
It scrapes 40 leads from Google Maps. But it's not collecting everything — it filters as it goes. No website? Discarded. Rating below 3.5 stars? Discarded. Already contacted before? Skipped. It only keeps companies worth talking to.

**Step 2 — Enrich**
For each company that passes the filter, the system visits their website — homepage, about page, contact page, team page — and reads them like a human would. It's looking for two things: a general contact email, and the name and title of whoever is actually in charge. CEO, Founder, Head of Operations — whoever it can find.

If it can't find a decision-maker email on the website itself, it doesn't give up. It runs through a cascade of five professional email-finding services — Hunter.io, Snov.io, Tomba.io, GetProspect, and Prospeo — querying each one in order of quality until it finds a verified email address. Five different services, all on free tiers, stacked together to maximise coverage without spending a cent.

**Step 3 — Research**
Now it knows who the company is. It hands that information to Claude — Anthropic's AI — and asks it to think like a consultant. Given what this company does, what specific automation could actually help them? Not generic pitches. Concrete ones. "Build an n8n workflow to automate their client onboarding". "Create an AI chatbot to handle first-response customer support." Claude reads the company summary and generates 2 to 4 real, actionable opportunities.

If Claude can't find anything worth pitching, the company is quietly skipped. No wasted emails.

**Step 4 — Write**
For companies that have real opportunities, Claude writes two completely different cold emails — one to the general company inbox, one directly to the decision maker by name. Both are plain text, personalised to the company, reference what they actually do, and pitch a specific solution. No buzzwords. No "I hope this email finds you well." Just a direct, human-sounding message from a fellow professional.

**Step 5 — Send**
The emails go out through Gmail using OAuth2 — the same way a real person would send them. The system respects a 30-emails-per-day cap to protect inbox reputation and avoid spam filters. If the daily limit is hit mid-run, remaining companies are queued and processed first the next morning before new leads are added.

Every lead is tracked in Airtable — enriched, researched, drafted, sent, queued, failed. Nothing gets lost. No company is ever contacted twice.

When the run is done, a summary arrives on Telegram: how many leads were discovered, enriched, researched, drafted, saved, and sent. All while the laptop was closed.

### The Infrastructure

The whole system runs on Heroku — a cloud server that keeps it alive 24/7. A singleton guard ensures only one instance ever runs at a time, preventing duplicate pipelines from draining API credits. The Telegram bot uses long polling so it's always listening, even on a server with no public IP address.

The tech stack: Node.js for the core engine, Puppeteer for all browser automation, Claude API for AI research and email writing, Gmail API for sending, Airtable as the CRM, Telegraf for the Telegram bot, and five email-finding APIs working in a waterfall cascade.

### What It Actually Does

Every single day, automatically, this system finds 40 potential clients across 18 countries, filters them down to the legitimate ones, reads their websites, finds the right person to email, researches a specific reason to reach out, writes two personalised emails, sends them, tracks everything, and reports back.

No virtual assistant. No manual sourcing. No copy-paste. Just results in the inbox every morning.

## Project 3: SaaS Lifecycle Engine

This is a productized backend service built in Node.js that any SaaS company can plug their product into via API. The core idea: it watches user behavior, computes scores, and automatically sends the right message to the right user at the right lifecycle stage — without the client having to build any of that logic themselves.

### The Architecture

**Tech stack:** Express.js server → PostgreSQL (data) → Redis + BullMQ (job queue) → Resend API (emails) → Railway (hosting)

**How it works end-to-end:**
- A client SaaS app sends events to POST /events (e.g., core_feature.used, payment.failed)
- The engine scores each user across multiple dimensions
- BullMQ jobs run on schedule (hourly or daily) to recalculate scores and fire triggers
- When conditions are met, the engine sends emails via Resend and/or fires webhooks back to the client's own URL
- Everything is deduplicated at the database level — no double emails, no double triggers

### The 5 Skills (Lifecycle Stages)

**Skill 1 — Onboarding (Days 0–14)**
Tracks a setup score (0–100) based on actions like verifying email, completing profile, inviting teammates. Fires a 7-email sequence based on score thresholds and time-since-signup. Detects "stalled" users who go quiet early and intervenes.

**Skill 2 — Activation (Days 0–28 or until score ≥ 80)**
Tracks an activation score based on feature adoption (core feature, power feature, sticky feature, return visits). Detects the "aha moment" (score ≥ 40 + power feature used), flags Product Qualified Leads (PQLs) at score ≥ 70 with buying signals, and fires feature adoption emails.

**Skill 3 — Retention (Day 14+, all non-churned users)**
Computes a health score across 5 weighted dimensions: usage frequency, feature depth, outcome achievement, collaboration, and support sentiment. Applies a decay penalty for inactivity (−5 pts/day inactive). Runs a re-engagement waterfall — escalating emails at days 3, 7, 14, 21, 28 of inactivity.

**Skill 4 — Expansion & Upsell (Healthy users: health ≥ 60 + activation ≥ 60)**
Computes an expansion score based on signals like hitting usage ceilings, feature gates, and team growth. Assigns a readiness tier (not_ready, warming, ready, urgent). Fires upsell emails for ready/urgent tiers.

**Skill 5 — Winback & Churn Recovery (Churned or at-risk users)**
Computes a churn prediction score based on login drops, support tickets, inactivity, pricing page visits, low NPS. Handles a full cancellation flow (exit survey → confirm churn → log to churn_log). Queues a 5-email winback sequence as delayed BullMQ jobs.

### The Business Model

This is designed as a white-label B2B service. Each client authenticates with an API key, has their own users/events isolated by client_id, and provides a webhook_url where the engine can push signals (PQL flagged, champion detected, CSM alert). The client never has to build lifecycle logic — they just send events and the engine handles the rest.

### The 5 Skills (Lifecycle Stages)

The engine covers a user's entire lifespan inside a SaaS product:

**Skill 1 — Onboarding (Days 0–14)**
Tracks a setup score (0–100) based on actions like verifying email, completing profile, inviting teammates. Fires a 7-email sequence based on score thresholds and time-since-signup. Detects "stalled" users who go quiet early and intervenes.

**Skill 2 — Activation (Days 0–28 or until score ≥ 80)**
Tracks an activation score based on feature adoption (core feature, power feature, sticky feature, return visits). Detects the "aha moment" (score ≥ 40 + power feature used), flags Product Qualified Leads (PQLs) at score ≥ 70 with buying signals, and fires feature adoption emails.

**Skill 3 — Retention (Day 14+, all non-churned users)**
Computes a health score across 5 weighted dimensions: usage frequency, feature depth, outcome achievement, collaboration, and support sentiment. Applies a decay penalty for inactivity (−5 pts/day inactive). Runs a re-engagement waterfall — escalating emails at days 3, 7, 14, 21, 28 of inactivity, then a webhook to the client's CSM team at day 30. Sets/unsets an at_risk_flag on the user record.

**Skill 4 — Expansion & Upsell (Healthy users: health ≥ 60 + activation ≥ 60)**
Computes an expansion score based on signals like hitting usage ceilings, feature gates, and team growth. Assigns a readiness tier (not_ready, warming, ready, urgent). Fires upsell emails for ready/urgent tiers, sends a monthly-to-annual conversion offer for long-tenured healthy users, and detects champion users (fires webhook to client).

**Skill 5 — Winback & Churn Recovery (Churned or at-risk users)**
Computes a churn prediction score based on login drops, support tickets, inactivity, pricing page visits, low NPS. Handles a full cancellation flow (exit survey → confirm churn → log to churn_log). Determines winback eligibility and queues a 5-email winback sequence as delayed BullMQ jobs (spread over 7→30→60→90→180 days). Also handles involuntary churn — a payment.failed event triggers a 4-email payment recovery sequence that gets cancelled if payment.recovered comes in.
